What is air-handwriting?

Air-handwriting consists of two levels: motion characters and motion words.  Motion characters are isolated alphanumeric letters and written in a uni-stroke style.  A motion word is formed by connecting motion characters with ligature motion in between.  The writing rendered in the air is uni-stroke without pen-up and pen-down information.  The user writes in a virtual box for every character.

Motion Characters

We recorded uppercase A to Z for motion characters.  Each character is repeated 10 times for every subject.  To eliminate allographs or different stroke orders, we instructed the subject to follow certain “stroke order” for each character as follows.

Motion Words

There are 40 words in our vocabulary, which include common TV channels and digital/Internet services.  Each word is repeated 5 times for every subject.  We divide them into four sets.  Each set has 10 words with an average of four characters per word.
